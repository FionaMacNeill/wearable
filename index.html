Research Team

Theo Fotis's bio goes here...

Fiona MacNeill is a part-time MSc User Experience Design student, focusing on the variance between users’ perceptual experience of technology and the operational nature of its use; studying the idiomatic culture used to coalesce these two states. She received her BA(hons) Fine Art from University of Kent in 2005, moving into creative digital media and then computing in 2009. MacNeill’s professional practice is founded on user-focused, design-grounded theory, coupled with consideration of privacy and trust issues, particularly in IoT, mobile and cloud computing.

Hand/Wrist Type

This is where I need to provide a brief description of the different types of devices that we have been looking at and the types of sensors that they contain. I think that including a list of Pros and Cons.

Pros:

  • Readily visible to the user at all times
  • Popular device types are available
  • Where the device has an touchscreen interface or physical buttons this could be used for self-reporting

Cons:

  • The patient may not wish for others to know about their use of the device
  • The device may not be in-keeping with the user's aesthetic taste
  • The device may be inconvenient when undertaking certain physical activities

Clip-on/Fasten Type

This is where I need to provide a brief description of the different types of devices that we have been looking at and the types of sensors that they contain. I think that including a list of Pros and Cons.

Pros:

  • More discreet and can be hidden under clothing
  • Perceived as less of a sport or fitness device
  • Where the device has an touchscreen interface or physical buttons this could be used for self-reporting

Cons:

  • The user may forget to wear/clip on the device
  • The users phone would need to be the main interface, if an interface for the patient is a requirement
  • The device may be inconvenient when undertaking certain physical activities
